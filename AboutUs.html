About Warner Bros.

Our Legacy

Warner Bros. has a rich legacy that spans nearly a century of shaping the entertainment industry. Founded in 1923 by Harry, Albert, Sam, and Jack Warner, the studio quickly became a powerhouse in Hollywood. From its early days of pioneering sound cinema with The Jazz Singer to its modern blockbusters, Warner Bros. has continuously pushed the boundaries of storytelling and filmmaking.

Throughout its history, Warner Bros. has introduced iconic characters like Superman, Batman, and Harry Potter, captivating audiences worldwide. The studio's diverse portfolio includes timeless classics such as Casablanca, groundbreaking franchises like Harry Potter, and contemporary hits like The Dark Knight trilogy.

Our Vision

At Warner Bros., our vision is to continue inspiring and entertaining audiences across the globe. We remain committed to innovation and creativity, exploring new storytelling mediums and technologies to deliver unforgettable experiences.

Fun Facts

  • Did you know? The iconic Warner Bros. water tower isn't just a set piece; it's a functioning water tower that still serves the studio lot.
  • Voice actor Mel Blanc, known as the "Man of a Thousand Voices," voiced beloved characters like Bugs Bunny and Daffy Duck. He is buried in Hollywood Forever Cemetery with the epitaph: "That's All Folks!"
  • Before becoming "Warner Bros.," the brothers' original surname was "Wonskolaser."
